## 1. What makes this dataset actually valuable (the asset)

Not the individual numbers — those are mostly public. The value is in **four things nobody else
has assembled for Borneo:**

1. **Cross-border unification.** Sabah + Sarawak (Malaysia) + Brunei + Kalimantan (5 Indonesian
   provinces) stitched into *one comparable layer*, harmonised across 3 jurisdictions and 3
   geographic levels. Deforestation, palm-oil pressure and resilience shifts **leak across these
   borders** — and only a unified Borneo view can see the leakage. Governments each see their own
   half; we see the island.
2. **Provenance as a first-class field.** Every value carries `data_level` + `confidence` +
   source + "never imputed." In compliance and credit markets, *auditability is the product.*
3. **A synthesis metric — the Resilience Index / True Wealth Hexagon.** Turns raw indicators into
   a decision-ready 0–100 score with the weakest pillar surfaced. That's the differentiated
   "brand" nobody else offers.
4. **A decision tool, not just a mirror — the Development Impact Simulator.** "What happens to
   Borneo's true wealth if we build +50,000 ha of plantation here?" moves the product from
   passive monitoring to active due-diligence.

## 2. The three markets it sits on (why now)

### A. EUDR — regulation with a hard deadline (the sharpest near-term wedge)
From **30 December 2026**, palm oil, timber, rubber, cocoa, soy, cattle, coffee placed on or
exported from the EU must be proven **deforestation-free**, with **plot-level geolocation
coordinates + proof of legal production**, filed as a Due Diligence Statement in TRACES. Borneo —
especially Indonesian Borneo — is a named deforestation hotspot, and **smallholder aggregation is
the industry's unsolved problem.** This is *mandatory, dated demand* for exactly the data we track.

### B. Carbon & biodiversity credits / digital-MRV (the scale play)
Southeast Asia (Indonesia-led, ~749 MtCO₂e; ~1.27 **billion** potential credits) is the world's
largest nature-based supply. Modern **dMRV = satellite + AI + IoT + blockchain** — cuts MRV cost
**50–70%**, enables frequent, tamper-proof, tokenised (RWA) issuance. **This is ABCDE, feature for
feature.** Our forest-cover / deforestation / fire layers are core MRV inputs.

### C. ESG / natural-capital intelligence (the platform market)
The ESG-data market is ~**$5B (2025)**; startups now hold a third of it. Every source agrees the
**#1 moat is data quality / provenance / reproducible methodology / transparent scoring** — i.e.
our E. Buyers: investors, banks, insurers, governments pricing natural-capital risk.

## 4. Selling points mapped to A · B · C · D · E

- **D — the data is the asset.** The only unified, per-district, provenance-tagged Borneo
  natural-capital + resilience layer. *Sell:* dataset access + the Resilience Index.
- **E — provenance is the moat.** `data_level` + confidence + never-imputed = the exact thing that
  kills buyer skepticism in compliance/credit markets. *Sell:* "audit-ready by design." **Invest here first.**
- **C — freshness is the premium.** Live satellite/IoT feeds turn an annual report into an
  auditable, frequent monitor — the dMRV requirement. *Sell:* alerting, near-real-time deforestation watch.
- **A — intelligence is the leverage.** The Impact Simulator (due-diligence) + AI plot aggregation
  (EUDR's hard problem) + AI extraction (lower cost). *Sell:* decision-support, not just dashboards.
- **B — tokenisation is the endgame.** Immutable DDS / registry, tokenised deforestation-free &
  carbon/biodiversity credits as RWAs, **yield returning to communities.** *Sell (later):* the
  marketplace + registry fees. **Narrate as horizon; never claim as built.**

## 5. Patterns the dataset is uniquely able to surface (how to investigate it)

These are the analyses that turn raw data into a sellable signal — and they showcase the thesis:

1. **Deforestation hotspots vs concession boundaries** → an EUDR *risk score* per supplier plot.
2. **Cross-border leakage** — deforestation/pressure migrating between the 4 jurisdictions (only a
   unified Borneo layer sees this).
3. **Resilience decline vs development pressure** — the Impact Simulator's before/after; "harm > benefit → red alert."
4. **Pillar fragility** — Brunei is paper-rich but ~8.4% food-self-sufficient → *fragile* true
   wealth. The single sharpest illustration of "paper wealth ≠ true wealth" — lead demos with it.

## 6. Recommended wedge (satisfies the user, meets the requirements, is ABCDE-complete)

**Position Borneo Tracker as the "Borneo True-Wealth & Deforestation Intelligence" layer, and
enter through the EUDR/deforestation door.** Why this one:

- **Uses data we already have** (deforestation, forest cover, fire, per-district) — no new pipeline.
- **A concrete, dated, mandatory payer** (EUDR 2026) — real business logic, not hand-waving.
- **Naturally stages every ABCDE letter** as an honest roadmap the supervisor will recognise:
  - **Now (ship honestly):** **D** (the unified dataset) + **E** (provenance = the compliance moat) + the Resilience Index brand.
  - **Next:** **C** (satellite freshness / alerts) + **A** (Impact Simulator + AI plot aggregation).
  - **Horizon:** **B** (on-chain DDS + tokenised deforestation-free / carbon credits, community yield).
- **Still meets the academic brief** (map, ESG, SDG, RAG dashboard, admin, simulator) — the wedge
  is a *framing* of the required product, not a replacement.
- **Embodies the book:** measuring, protecting, and monetising *true wealth* — with the Impact
  Simulator as the flagship "protect true wealth" moment.

**One-sentence pitch to the supervisor:** *"We built the D and E of your framework as a real,
provenance-first Borneo natural-capital dataset; EUDR gives it a paying customer next year; and
A-C-B are the roadmap that turns the measure into a market — with the yield returning to Borneo's
communities."*
