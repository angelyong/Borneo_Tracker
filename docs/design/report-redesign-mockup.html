Overview

Executive Summary

This profile compiles 20 environmental, social and governance indicators for Sabah from official sources — the World Bank, Malaysia's OpenDOSM, Global Forest Watch, UNESCO and state agencies. Environmentally, Sabah remains heavily forested but has lost about 1.8 million hectares of tree cover since 2000 (~27% of its baseline), with 697 fire alerts in 2025 — the key signals for deforestation and haze due diligence. Socially, near-universal basics sit alongside real gaps: 80.5% clean-water access, 5.7% unemployment, and a 17.7% absolute poverty rate — the highest of any Malaysian state. Governance is captured by a single national indicator (Control of Corruption, 57.9 / 100).

  • Deforestation is the headline environmental signal — 1.8M ha lost since 2000 (~27% of baseline), plus 697 fire alerts in 2025.
  • Poverty is the headline social gap — 17.7%, the highest in Malaysia; unemployment (5.7%) is also above the ~3% national rate.
  • Coverage is deep on Social (13 indicators) but thin on Governance (1) — detailed in Coverage & Limitations below.
  • Some figures are national or hand-entered — Control of Corruption is a national score applied to Sabah; life expectancy and tourist arrivals come from cited releases, not automated feeds.

Honesty

Coverage & Limitations

Exactly what this profile does not cover, and which figures to treat with care — so a gap is never mistaken for a zero.

Gaps in coverage

  • Governance is thin — a single indicator (Control of Corruption), and it's a national figure, not state-specific. No state-level governance data yet.
  • No emissions / greenhouse-gas indicator — climate coverage is currently fire, air quality and forest-loss proxies only.
  • Several social figures are absolute counts, not rates — electricity access, school enrolment, hospital beds and households are volumes; without population normalisation they aren't directly comparable to other territories.

Measured but flagged

  • Manual Life expectancy, tourist arrivals and national parks are hand-entered from cited government / press releases, not automated feeds.
  • Medium Air quality is one live city station; Control of Corruption and renewable-share are national proxies applied to the state.
  • Mixed vintages — figures range from a 2000 forest baseline to 2026 live readings; each row shows its own year rather than a single "as of" date.
